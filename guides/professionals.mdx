### Meeting Notes & Follow-ups

Capture action items as they happen:

> "Meeting notes from the product review: John to finalize the roadmap by Friday. Marketing team will prepare launch materials. Engineering needs two more weeks for the API integration. Next meeting scheduled for March 15th."

### Executive Communications

Draft thoughtful responses to board members, investors, or partners:

> "Thank you for your continued support and partnership. We're excited to share that Q4 exceeded our projections by 15%. The team has been working diligently on expanding our enterprise offerings, and we expect to see continued momentum in the coming quarter."

### Performance Reviews

Write comprehensive feedback efficiently:

> "Alex has demonstrated exceptional leadership this quarter. He successfully led the product launch, mentored two junior team members, and consistently met all deadlines. Areas for growth include expanding cross-functional collaboration. Overall rating: Exceeds expectations."

### Strategic Documents

Draft proposals, memos, and plans:

> "Proposal to expand into the European market. Key considerations: regulatory compliance, local partnerships, pricing strategy. Recommended approach: Start with UK and Germany, establish local entity within 6 months, target enterprise customers initially."

## Professional Communication Tips

### Maintain Your Tone

Ella captures exactly what you say. Speak as professionally as you'd write:

| Casual | Professional |
|--------|--------------|
| "Hey, got your note" | "Thank you for your message" |
| "Sounds good" | "I agree with your proposed approach" |
| "ASAP" | "At your earliest convenience" |

### Structure Your Thoughts First

Before dictating longer content, mentally outline:

1. Opening/context
2. Main points
3. Call to action/next steps
4. Closing

### Use Natural Punctuation

> "Dear Board Members comma. I'm pleased to report that our annual revenue grew by 25 percent period. This exceeds our initial projections significantly period."

## Workflow Optimization

### Morning Email Routine

1. Open inbox
2. Triage: Archive, delete, or queue for response
3. Use Ella to rapidly respond to queued emails
4. Inbox zero in 30 minutes vs. 2 hours

### Meeting Preparation

1. Dictate agenda items as you think of them
2. Capture pre-meeting thoughts
3. Draft key points you want to make

### Post-Meeting Follow-up

1. Immediately after meeting ends
2. Dictate summary and action items while fresh
3. Send follow-up emails same-day

## Best Practices for Professionals

### 1. Prepare Context Before Dictating

Have relevant information (names, numbers, dates) ready before you start.

### 2. Use Toggle Mode for Longer Content

For emails and documents over a paragraph, Toggle mode lets you think and speak naturally.

### 3. Review Before Sending

Quick proofread catches any transcription variations before important communications go out.

### 4. Batch Similar Tasks

Dictate all your emails at once, then all your meeting notes, to stay in flow.

### 5. Add Industry Terms to Dictionary

Add company names, product names, and industry jargon:

```
Q3/Q4
ROI
KPI
YoY
MoM
SaaS
B2B
P&L
EBITDA
```
